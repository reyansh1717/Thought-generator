Today starts the 9-day-long festival of navratri. Navratri signifies 9 vibrant nights of devotion, love, sprituality and garba. May maa ambe protect us always. Jai mata di, shubh navratri. Day 1: Maa Shailaputri (Beginning navratri with the grace of maa shailaputri) Daughter of the himalayas, riding nandi with a trident and lotus. Symbol of strength and new beginnings. Day 2: Maa brahmacharini (she blesses those who choose truth and discipline) The goddess of penance and devotion, holding a rosary and kamandal. Embodiment of discipline and faith. Durga maa will protect u (my girl) from anything bad around u. Day 3: Maa chandraghanta (courage shines where maa chandraghanta blesses) Fierce warrior godddess with a crescent moon on her forehead, riding a tiger. Symbol of courage and protection. Day 4: Maa Kushmanda (The cosmic mother blessed with health, wealth, and positivity) Creator of universe, radiating energgy and light, seated on a lion with eight hands. Bringer of health and positivity. Day 5: Maa Skandamata (Worshiping her is worshiping motherhood itself) mother of kartikeya,, seated on a lion with her child on her lap. symbol of motherly love and compassion. Day 6: Maa Katyayani (she is destroyer of injustice and darkness) Warrior goddess born to stage katyayan, riding a lion with weapons in her hands. Destroyer of evil and protector of dharma. Day 7: Maa Kaalratri (Her roar terrifies demons, her love protects devotees) Dark and fierce form, with disheveled hair and riding a donkey. Destroyer of demons and remover of fear. Day 8: Maa Mahagauri (She cleanses sins and grants spiritual strenght) Fair and serene goddess dressed in white, riding a bull. Symbol of purity, peace and forgiveness.
